Historical Footprint: From the Bow to Geometry

Did you know? Thousands of years ago, ancient Greek mathematicians observed familiar everyday objects to build the first building blocks of Geometry.

  • 🏹 The word "Arc" comes from the Latin word "arcus", which literally means a bow used for shooting arrows.
  • 🧶 The word "Chord" comes from the Latin word "chorda", referring to the string used for bows or ancient lutes.

In modern mathematical language, if we consider the curved wooden body as a part of a circle, then the straight string connecting its two ends is the perfect image of a chord!

Concepts of Chord and Diameter

  • Chord: A line segment connecting two distinct points on the circle.
    Example: Line segment $AB$ is a chord of circle $(O)$.
  • Diameter: A chord that passes through the center of the circle. It is the longest chord of a circle.
    Example: Line segment $CD$ passing through the center $O$ is a diameter.

Discovering Theorems

The diameter is a special chord because it passes through the center of the circle. The diameter is the longest chord in any circle.

Theorem (Perpendicular relationship between diameter and chord):

In a circle, a diameter perpendicular to a chord bisects that chord (passes through its midpoint).

If diameter $CD \perp$ chord $AB$ at $H$, then $HA = HB$.

Central Angles & Arc Measures

Central Angle:

An angle whose vertex is the center of the circle.
Example: $\angle AOB$ is a central angle subtending arc $AB$.

Arc Measure:
  • The measure of a minor arc is equal to the measure of its corresponding central angle: $m\overparen{AB} = \angle AOB$
  • The measure of a major arc is $360^\circ$ minus the measure of the minor arc.
  • The measure of a semicircle is $180^\circ$.

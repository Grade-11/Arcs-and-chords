Historical Footprint: From the Bow to Geometry

Did you know? Thousands of years ago, ancient Greek mathematicians observed familiar everyday objects to build the first building blocks of Geometry.

  • 🏹 The word "Arc" comes from the Latin word "arcus", which literally means a bow used for shooting arrows.
  • 🧶 The word "Chord" comes from the Latin word "chorda", referring to the string used for bows or ancient lutes.

In modern mathematical language, if we consider the curved wooden body as a part of a circle, then the straight string connecting its two ends is the perfect image of a chord!

Now that we know the history, let's explore the formal mathematical concepts!

Concepts of Chord and Diameter

  • Chord: A line segment connecting two distinct points on the circle.
    Example: Line segment $AB$ is a chord of circle $(O)$.
  • Diameter: A chord that passes through the centre of the circle. It is a special type of chord.
    Example: Line segment $CD$ passing through the centre $O$ is a diameter.

Formal Theorem Unlocked

Theorem: In a circle, the diameter is the longest chord.

Mathematical Proof Summary:

  • Let $AB$ be any chord in circle $(O, R)$.
  • Consider $\triangle OAB$. According to the triangle inequality theorem:
    $$AB \le OA + OB$$
  • Since $OA$ and $OB$ are radii, $OA = R$ and $OB = R$. Therefore:
    $$AB \le R + R = 2R$$
  • The equality $AB = 2R$ holds true only if points $A, O, B$ are collinear, which means chord $AB$ passes through the centre $O$ (making it a diameter).

Practice 1

Given a circle with diameter $BC$. Prove that for any point $A$ (distinct from $B$ and $C$) on the circle, we always have:

$$BC < AB + AC < 2BC$$

Instruction: Apply the theorems you have just learned to think through and write the solution to this exercise in your notebook!

The Solution is Locked

Your teacher will unlock the detailed solution and illustration after you have tried it yourself.

Detailed Solution Unlocked

Step-by-step Proof:

1. Prove $BC < AB + AC$:

  • Consider $\triangle ABC$. Since $A$ is a point on the circle distinct from $B$ and $C$, they form a valid triangle.
  • According to the Triangle Inequality Theorem, the sum of any two sides is always greater than the third side.
  • Therefore, $AB + AC > BC$ (which means $BC < AB + AC$).

2. Prove $AB + AC < 2BC$:

  • The diameter $BC$ is the longest chord in the circle.
  • Since $A$ is distinct from $B$ and $C$, chords $AB$ and $AC$ are not diameters.
  • Thus, $AB < BC$ and $AC < BC$.
  • Adding these inequalities together: $AB + AC < BC + BC = 2BC$.
Conclusion: Combining both parts, we get $BC < AB + AC < 2BC$.

Central Angles & Arc Measures

Central Angle:

An angle whose vertex is the centre of the circle.
Example: $\angle AOB$ is a central angle subtending arc $AB$.

Arc Measure:
  • The measure of a minor arc is equal to the measure of its corresponding central angle: $m\overparen{AB} = \angle AOB$
  • The measure of a major arc is $360^\circ$ minus the measure of the minor arc.
  • The measure of a semicircle is $180^\circ$.
